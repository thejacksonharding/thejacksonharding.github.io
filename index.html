About Me

Hi! I’m Jackson Harding, a third-year undergraduate student at the University of Calgary majoring in Biomedical Engineering with a minor in Digital Engineering (which focuses on programming, machine learning, and signal processing). This knowledge enables me to blend biology and technology to develop healthcare solutions that improve the quality of life for individuals facing medical challenges. Throughout my academic journey, I’ve gained hands-on experience in engineering principles, data analysis, and research design.

I am actively seeking a 12-to-16-month internship position starting in May 2025 to develop my industry experience and skills. Currently, I am working on a project that explores configurations for ultrasound neurostimulation, to improve the effectiveness and practicality of transducer designs for experimental application. I have also recently contributed to developing a diagnostic index to assess microbiome health in premature infants, utilizing molecular techniques and data analysis to identify key biomarkers related to gut health.

As the first person in my family to attend university, I am driven to positively impact my community and inspire others to pursue careers in science and engineering. In the summer of 2024, I presented my research at the Snyder Research Symposium, focusing on the impact of gut microbiome maturation on health outcomes for premature infants. This experience helped me recognize my passion for research and its potential to impact people’s lives.

I am actively seeking opportunities to engage with others in the biomedical field. I believe that the innovations in biology and technology are constantly evolving, and I am excited to contribute to a brighter future in these fields. Outside of my academic pursuits, I enjoy hands-on design and tinkering as well as solving puzzles. I also have a keen interest in reading, traveling, and topics such as personal development and learning about technology and artificial intelligence.
